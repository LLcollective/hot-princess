The Test of Compassion

Empathy is often selective, drawn to what’s familiar or easy to sympathize with. But compassion calls us deeper, to act even when it’s uncomfortable. That’s the real test of compassion: whether we extend it even when violence breaks the pattern people would rather project onto someone else, and the silence grows heavier, tilting toward avoidance. The imbalance becomes harder to ignore. How do we choose who deserves our empathy, and what does compassion look like when no one is left out?
