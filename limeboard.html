The Test of Compassion

Empathy often flows most easily toward what feels familiar or relatable. Compassion asks us to reach beyond that, to act with care even when it feels uncomfortable or unfamiliar. The real test of compassion is whether we extend it when silence feels easier than accountability, and when true inclusion asks us to look past what is convenient.

How do we decide who deserves our empathy, and what does compassion look like when no one is left out?
